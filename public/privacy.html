Our Privacy Commitment

QuickTLDR is a Chrome extension that uses artificial intelligence to summarize web content. Our privacy approach is simple: we minimize data handling. This document explains exactly what happens with your data when you use our extension.

Quick Summary: We don't sell your data. We don't track you. We don't show ads. We don't store your personal information on our servers. Your summaries stay on your device unless you choose to use cloud AI services.

1. Information We Collect

QuickTLDR is designed to collect minimal information necessary for functionality.

What We Process:

  • Webpage Text: When you request a summary, we process the text content of the current webpage. This text is sent to AI providers only for generating summaries and is not stored by us.
  • Optional API Key: If you choose to use your own AI API key, it's stored locally in your browser and transmitted only to the AI provider you've selected.
  • Local Settings: Your preferences (theme, default settings, etc.) are stored locally in your browser.

What We DO NOT Collect:

No personal identity information
No browsing history
No analytics or tracking
No advertising data
No location data
No demographic information

2. How Information Is Used

All data processing serves one purpose: providing the summarization feature.

1
Text Processing

Webpage text is processed either locally or sent to an AI service to generate a summary.

2
Local Storage

If Memory feature is enabled, summaries are stored only on your device in your browser's local storage.

3
Display

The generated summary is displayed to you within the extension interface.

No Secondary Uses: We don't use your data for marketing, advertising, profiling, or any purpose other than providing summarization functionality.

3. Data Storage

Local Storage (Your Device)

  • Memory Feature: If enabled, your summary history is stored exclusively in your browser's local storage. This data never leaves your computer.
  • Settings & API Keys: Your preferences and optional API keys are stored locally using Chrome's storage API.
  • Automatic Sync: If you have Chrome sync enabled, your local settings may sync across your devices via Google's sync service (subject to Google's privacy policy).

Our Servers

We operate no servers that store your personal data. We don't have databases of user information, browsing history, or summary content. There's nothing for us to secure because we don't collect it.

4. Third-Party Services

QuickTLDR may use third-party AI services to generate summaries. Here's how that works:

AI Service Providers

  • Default Service: When using our default AI service, webpage text is sent to our AI provider to generate summaries.
  • Your Own API Key: You can provide your own API key (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.). In this case, text is sent directly to that provider, and we never see or store your API key on our servers.
  • Provider Policies: When using any cloud AI service, your interaction is also subject to that provider's privacy policy. We encourage you to review their policies.

Offline/Local Mode

If you use an offline AI model or local processing mode, no data leaves your device. All processing happens locally on your computer.

5. Security

We implement security best practices appropriate to our minimal data handling:

  • No Data Collection: The most secure data is data we never collect. Since we don't store personal information on our servers, there's no database to breach.
  • Secure Connections: All communications with AI providers use HTTPS encryption.
  • Browser Security: Local data benefits from Chrome's built-in security features and sandboxing.
  • Regular Updates: We maintain the extension with security updates and follow Chrome extension security best practices.

6. User Control

You have complete control over your data and experience:

  • Disable Memory Feature: Turn off local storage of summaries in extension settings.
  • Clear Local Data: Remove all locally stored data via Chrome's extension management or browser data clearing.
  • Use Your Own API Key: Maintain control over which AI provider processes your data.
  • Offline Mode: Use local AI models to prevent any data from leaving your device.
  • Uninstall: Remove the extension at any time via Chrome's extension management. This removes all local data.
  • Review Permissions: QuickTLDR requests minimal permissions needed for functionality, which you can review at installation.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons.

  • We will post the updated policy on this page with a revised "Last Updated" date
  • For significant changes, we may provide additional notice within the extension
  • Continued use of QuickTLDR after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy

We encourage you to periodically review this page for the latest information on our privacy practices.
